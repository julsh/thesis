Barbados is an island nation located towards the east of the Caribbean Sea and in the west of the Atlantic Ocean, part of the eastern islands of the Lesser Antilles, with the nations of Saint Lucia and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines being its closest neighbours. The island is 430 square kilometres, (166 sq. mi), and is primarily low-lying, with some higher areas in the island's interior. It is located 13° north of the Equator and 59° west of the Prime Meridian, about 434.5 kilometres (270 mi) northeast of Venezuela. management.

Internet, Broadband and Consumer E-Commerce in Barbados

June 2009 Review

 Broadband connectivity is exploding across Barbados. Deputy Prime Minister, Mia Mottley says connections with Cable and Wireless alone have in the last two years grown from 14 hundred to 49 thousand. She was speaking at the launch of Connect Barbados where Cable and Wireless will be giving free high-speed internet access to several communities and institutions across Barbados.

President of Cable and Wireless, Donald Austin, says the second phase of Connect Barbados will offer free internet access to micro businesses. He explained that they will work with relevant ministries and agencies to determine the needs of the fledgling businesses.
