The Dominican Republic is a country located on the eastern two-thirds of the Caribbean island of Hispaniola, bordering Haiti. Hispaniola is the second-largest of the Greater Antilles islands, and lies west of Puerto Rico and east of Cuba and Jamaica. The Dominican Republic is not to be confused with Dominica, another Caribbean country. Once dependent on the export of sugar and other agricultural products, the DR has become the most popular tourist destination in the Caribbean. Tourism, and free-trade zones, have become major employers and key sources of revenue.

Internet Broadband and E-Commerce in the Dominican Republic - June 2011 Review

 The Dominican Republic has over 3,851,278 Internet users as of December, 2010. This figure corresponds to 505,423 household accounts and 117,508 at work accounts. Most of the subscribers use DSL for access, and 243,645 subscriptions correspond to mobile accounts (smart phones). With this accomplishment, the official government's growth target has been fully met as anticipated, according to Secretary of State and Presidente of Indotel (Dominican Telecommunications Institute) Jose Rafael Vargas.

Telephone density in the Dominican Republic is more than 65%, a very good figure anywhere in the world. Broadband DSL represents about 60% of the total Internet subscribers, as already mentioned.
