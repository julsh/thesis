Asian Internet Market Overview
This market report provides a comprehensive overview of the Internet segment across the various economies of Asia. There is considerable activity in the Internet online markets in Asia, mainly by the big players of North Asia, (South Korea, Japan, Hong Kong, etc), with a significant role also being played by some of the South East Asian countries (Singapore, Malaysia, etc). Of course, in terms of sheer Internet user numbers, China and India maintain a real presence, despite their modest user penetration figures. China surpassed the US in 2008 to become the largest nation of Internet users in the world. The report also includes a look at the broadband segment within these markets. Asian Mobile Telecommunications and Wireless Broadband Market
With more than 1.9 billion Asians using mobile phones in 2009, the region's mobile markets offer huge potential for mobile data services. This market report provides an overview of the Mobile Data and Wireless Broadband Market segment across the various markets of Asia. Some 34 Asian countries are covered in the report. Asian Telecommunications Infrastructure Report
Over a number of decades the economies of Asia have progressively built substantial fixed-line national networks followed by national mobile networks. In many of the developing nations of the region, the building of fixed-line infrastructure was not far advanced before it was overwhelmed by the introduction of mobile infrastructure. This has created the phenomenon of substitution in many of the markets of Asia (where mobile services perform the function of the non-existent fixed services.) Fixed infrastructure remains an important component in the overall development of the telecom sector in Asia. By June 2009, Asia had infrastructure in place supporting a total of more than 2.4 billion telephone subscribers; of these, around 570 million were fixed-line subscribers, the remainder of course being mobile subscribers.
