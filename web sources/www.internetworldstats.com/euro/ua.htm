Ukraine Telecommunications Reports
Ukraine Internet, Broadband & Convergence Overview & Statistics Report
Internet penetration in the Ukraine is low, due to the lack of telecom infrastructure, high fees and low PC ownership. ADSL services are available from a number of broadband ISPs although the cost of services has resulted in low take-up. The wireless broadband sector is seeing increased activity as the number of WiFi ISPs has increased, and a mobile operator plans to launch fixed-broadband services using frequencies previously used by analogue mobile services. WiMAX services have been launched. The cable operators have a significant presence throughout the country due to large number of operators although the lack of investment is preventing them from offering broadband and digital cable services. A new DTH platform has been launched and the Ukrainian broadcasting council has allocated Digital Terrestrial TV licences, opening the way for services to commence. See table of contents.

Ukraine - Key Statistics and Telecommunications Market
The Ukrainian telecoms market is evolving, with steady steps taken to liberalise the market. Alternative operators offer services although the incumbent is still very much the dominant player in the fixed-line market, which is characterised by a low but growing fixed-line penetration rate. A number of wireless local operators offer services, and a WiMAX service has been launched. Numerous attempts have been made to privatise the government’s stake in Ukrtelecom, with none successful. Drawing upon numerous statistics, this report provides a concise overview of the Ukrainian telecoms market, including brief profiles of major operators, telecom network infrastructure in the country and network developments which are underway. See summary.

Ukraine - Key Statistics and Telecommunications Market
Ukraine’s mobile market, like that of Russia, has seen strong growth although growth is slowing due to the maturing market. Services are offered by four major network operators, two of which dominate the market. A fifth operator, which offers services in some regions, has been awarded a licence to offer services nationally. Foreign operators have a significant presence in the market, particularly those from neighbouring Russia. With the market maturing, the mobile operators are experiencing falling ARPU levels and slower growth. Consequently increased activity is being seen in the mobile data market, with new services becoming available, and plans to launch additional new services. This report offers an informative introduction to the Ukrainian mobile market with the assistance of insightful statistics, introducing the major operators, mobile technologies used and industry developments. See summary.
