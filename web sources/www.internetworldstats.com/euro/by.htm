CIS Statistics
The Commonwealth of Independent States - CIS - was created in 1991 and today comprises Azerbaijan, Armenia, Belarus, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Moldova, Russia, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan and Ukraine. In September of 1993 the CIS States signed an Agreement on the creation of an Economic Union to form a common economic space grounded on the free movement of goods, services, labour force, capital; to elaborate coordinated monetary, tax, price, customs, external economic policy; to bring together methods of regulating economic activity and create favourable conditions for the development of direct production relations.

Belarus Telecommunications Market Reports

 Belarus Telecoms Market Overview & Statistics Report
This report provides an overview of the Belarus telecom sector, accompanied by relevant statistics and brief profiles of the main operators. Internet use is low compared to other Eastern European countries and broadband services are available. The market is characterised by a fixed-line sector with a regionally high teledensity rate and a rapidly growing mobile sector, comprised of three GSM operators and one CDMA operator, with all four operator expanding network coverage.
