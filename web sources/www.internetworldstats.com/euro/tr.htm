 Turkey Telecommunications Market Reports

 Turkey Key Statistics, Telecommunications Market Overview and Internet Report
The development in the Turkish telecoms market is increasing with the ending of fixed-line operator Turk Telekom’s monopoly and the commencement of incumbent privatisation. Upcoming negotiations with the EU will be a driver for further reform in the market. Customers are showing increased acceptance of new technologies as the broadband market has experienced phenomenal growth.

 Turkey Mobile Communications and Broadcasting Report
This report from Paul Budde describes the somewhat volatile Turkish mobile communications market and also gives a brief description of the broadcasting market. Dominant operator Turkcell is experiencing difficulties in its expansion into other countries. Number two operator Telsim has extreme legal and political problems. The number three and four operators are in the process of merging. There are large numbers of both free-to-air and pay TV operators. Cable TV is widespread with operators having revenue sharing agreements with Turk Telekom.

 Middle East Internet and Broadband Report
This annual report provides an insight into the developing Broadband and Internet markets of the Middle East. Topics detailed in the report include the following: Regional Internet and broadband market developments and analysis, xDSL, Cable Modems, Fibre-to-the-home (FTTH), WiFi, Growth in regional broadband penetration and availability (Trends, Growth Patterns, Prospects), Major Broadband Service Providers, Internet Access Locations and ISPs, Censorship of the Internet in Middle East countries. Counties covered are: Bahrain, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Syria, Turkey, United Arab Emirates, Yemen
