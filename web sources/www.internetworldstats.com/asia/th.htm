Thailand - officially the Kingdom of Thailand is a country in Southeast Asia, bordering Laos and Cambodia to the east, the Gulf of Thailand and Malaysia to the south, and the Andaman Sea and Myanmar to the west. The country's official name was Siam, until 1939. The word Thai means "freedom" in the Thai language and is also the name of the majority ethnic group.

Thailand Telecommunications and Broadband Reports

Thailand Internet Market Overview and Statistics Report
Thailand has started to energetically embrace Internet in its many forms across the marketplace. Over the last five years, admittedly starting from a very low base, the estimated number of Internet users in Thailand has increased sixfold. ISPs also seem to be recovering from their earlier reputation for unreliable performance. Now that the government taken the first steps to fix the regulatory regime, with the CAT being sidelined from the activities of the ISPs, there should be more opportunity for the Internet business in Thailand to grow. This report looks at the Thai Internet market and some of the major players involved. See summary and table of contents


Thailand Telecommunications Infrastructure Report
Thailand has dedicated a substantial amount of its resources to the building of a national telecommunications infrastructure over the last decade or so. The economic crisis of the late 1990s was certainly a setback to this long term process. Nevertheless, the country has recovered much of the earlier momentum and has been attracting foreign capital and expertise once more to assist in the continuing process of expanding and integrating its networks. This report looks at where the industry is at the moment in terms of infrastructure and reviews the status of the major players involved in the building and the operating of the telecommunications sector. See summary and table of contents


Thailand Broadband Market Report
Thailand would appear to have shrugged off its earlier hesitation about the Internet. It has been energetically embracing everything online in the marketplace. After a period of heavy dependence on dial-up, there are the first signs of a serious push into broadband services. Most significantly, some of the players are committing significant capital to build infrastructure. And, with the government having finally put the new regulator in place, a start has been made on restructuring the Internet market. Once the government's changes to the regulatory regime start to impact, there should be more opportunity for broadband Internet to flourish. This report looks at the Thai broadband Internet market. See summary and table of contents

Broadband and Consumer E-Commerce in Thailand

December 2006 Review

 The future of E-Commerce in Thailand is positive. Both the government and business sectors are well positioned to experience the benefits of E-Commerce on a significant scale. Thailand government's ICT plan for 2000-2010 plays an important role in building an environment that helps E-Commerce by identifying and developing key building blocks, and enhancing the electronic environment for trading and transactions among businesses. The information infrasturcture is becoming more universal and less expensive. Security, privacy protection, and trust are being enhanced so that consumers will have more trust and confidence, and will try different products and services in the future. These developments are creating great opportunities E-Commerce solution providers, and E-Commerce-related service.
