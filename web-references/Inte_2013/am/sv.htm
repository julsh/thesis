El Salvador Telecommunications Report

El Salvador’s telecom market is among the most liberalised in Central America. Since the sector was privatised and opened to competition in 1998, foreign operators and local companies have invested millions in infrastructure improvements. However, the country experienced a couple of serious set backs due to natural disasters: Hurricane Mitch in 1998 and the devastating earthquakes of 2001. As a result, fixed-line teledensity is still only just over 11%, while mobile telephony overtook fixed lines in service in 2002, reaching around 19.3% penetration in 2004. The government’s liberal approach has allowed a promising diversity of telecom services to develop.
