 French Polynesia
The term French Polynesia is applied to the scattered French possessions in the
South Pacific, it includes Mangareva (Gambier), Makatea, the Marquesas Islands,
Rapa, Rurutu, Rimatara, the Society Islands, the Tuamotu Archipelago, Tubuai,
Raivavae, and the island of Clipperton. There are 120 islands, of which 25 are
uninhabited. The principal and most populous island is Tahiti.
